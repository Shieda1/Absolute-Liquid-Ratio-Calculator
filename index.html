What is the absolute liquid ratio?
The absolute liquid ratio is a ratio that measures the total liquidity that is available to the company. It considers the cash available to the firm and the marketable securities?
How to calculate absolute liquid ratio?
To calculate absolute liquid ratio you should use the following formula:
absolute liquid ratio = absolute liquid assets / current liabilities
example: company x has $2,050 worth of absolute liquid assets and it also has $7,960, calculate the absolute liquid ratio?
Using the absolute liquid ratio formula the asnwer will be:
absolute liquid ratio = absolute liquid assets / current liabilities
absolute liquid ratio = $2,050 / $7,960
absolute liquid ratio = 0.25
Why use absolute liquid ratio?
The absolute liquid ratio is used to measure the liquidity available for the frim because it determines how ease the company can pay it's short term liabilities.
